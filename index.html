Autonomous Environmental Survey Boat

Jan 2026 — Present
MicroPythonRaspberry Pi Pico GPS / CompassHC-12 RadioSensor Systems

An autonomous catamaran built from scratch to monitor water quality in local lakes and reservoirs. GPS waypoint navigation, compass heading control, and a 30-foot motorized sensor deployment winch enable fully autonomous environmental survey missions.

Catamaran hull design — twin pontoon configuration for stability

The dual-hull design provides exceptional stability on open water, critical for accurate sensor readings. Each pontoon houses waterproof compartments for batteries and electronics.

The modular waterproof sensor pods measure temperature, salinity, light penetration, and depth. Pods connect to the main bus via waterproof magnetic connectors, enabling quick field swaps without tools.

Sensor pod assembly with waterproof connectors

The control system runs on dual Raspberry Pi Pico microcontrollers — one dedicated to navigation and motor control, the other managing sensor acquisition and data logging.

The picos communicate over a shared UART bus, while the shore station receives real-time telemetry via an HC-12 433 MHz radio link with a range of over 1 km across open water.

Electronics bay — dual Pico control system
Shore station dashboard showing real-time GPS track and sensor data
Winch mechanism
GPS module mount
Water testing deployment

The full firmware stack is written in MicroPython with mission planning, geo-fenced safety boundaries, automatic return-to-home on low battery, and range estimation based on current draw and remaining capacity.

The project is ongoing, with plans to add dissolved oxygen sensing, current profiling, and multi-boat swarm coordination for larger survey areas.

Combat Robotics — Team Dihydrogen Monoxide

Mar 2023 — Jun 2025
CADCNC Machining 3D PrintingCompetition

Founded and led Team Dihydrogen Monoxide through two years of competitive combat robotics. Designed and fabricated 4 robots across 5 weight classes, earning Top 50 national placements and 16+ podium finishes at regional and national events.

Full robot lineup — antweight through beetleweight

Every robot was designed from scratch in CAD, with custom weapon systems optimized through iterative simulation and real-world testing. Weapon types included horizontal spinners, vertical drums, and overhead hammers.

Manufacturing combined CNC-machined aluminum frames with 3D-printed armor panels in reinforced nylon and polycarbonate. Critical joints used press-fit bushings and custom fastener patterns.

Competed at the 2024 and 2025 Robot Combat League National Championships. Achieved 4th place nationally in Plastic Antweight at the 2024 event — the team's best national result.

Across 5 regional competition events, the team earned 8 podium finishes including multiple 1st-place results. Each competition informed the next generation of designs through post-match damage analysis.

Autonomous Maze-Solving Robot

Oct 2025 — Dec 2025
OnshapeMicroPython AsyncioPathfinding

Led the design and programming of an autonomous maze-navigation robot for Northeastern's Cornerstone of Engineering course. Full ownership of mechanical CAD and the entire MicroPython firmware stack, from sensor fusion to real-time pathfinding.

Complete robot assembly with sensor array and differential drive

The robot's chassis was designed in Onshape with a focus on compactness and low center of gravity. The differential drive system used micro gear motors with optical encoders for precise odometry.

Five IR distance sensors provided a 180-degree forward view, with two side-mounted sensors for wall tracking. Sensor readings were fused using a weighted averaging algorithm to reject noise.

Onshape CAD model — exploded view

The asyncio-based control architecture ran sensor polling, motor control, and navigation logic as concurrent tasks on a single Raspberry Pi Pico. This enabled responsive obstacle avoidance without blocking the main navigation loop.

Navigation strategy combined right-wall-following with shortest-path optimization. The robot built an internal map of visited cells and could backtrack to explore unexplored branches.

The modular code architecture allowed rapid iteration on PID tuning and navigation strategies during testing sessions. Configurable parameters were exposed via a serial debug interface.

The project demonstrated the power of concurrent embedded programming for real-time robotics — a pattern I've since applied to the autonomous survey boat's control system.

Ceramic Tools R&D

Oct 2023 — May 2025
CAD3D PrintingDesign for Manufacturing

A research and development project creating specialized 3D-printed tools for ceramics education. Over 10 custom tools designed, prototyped, and deployed at Burlingame High School, with designs published online for the maker community.

Complete tool set — trimming tools, ribs, wire guides, and custom stamps

Each tool went through 3-5 design iterations, testing ergonomics and durability with ceramics students and teachers. Grip profiles were refined using hand measurements and clay resistance testing.

Print orientation and infill strategies were optimized for each tool's primary stress direction. PETG was selected for its chemical resistance to clay slip and glaze materials.

CAD models showing iteration history

Tools were designed with ease of printing in mind — minimal supports, flat bases for bed adhesion, and wall thicknesses optimized for 0.4mm nozzles. This made them accessible to anyone with a basic FDM printer.

All final designs were published online with detailed print settings, material recommendations, and usage guides. The files have been downloaded by educators and makers around the world.

This project taught me that great design serves its users. The most successful tools weren't the most complex — they were the ones that felt right in a potter's hand and solved a real frustration in the studio.
